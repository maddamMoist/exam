Reactive Attachment Disorder

A disorder, evident before age 5, where a child lacks emotional attachment to their care giver. They dont seek respond to comfort from them. It also requires a history of neglectful or unstable parenting that is presumed to be responsible. Autism excludes this dx. Its specified as persistent if it lasts more than 12 months. Developmental age must be 9+

Disinhibted Social Engagement Disorder

A disorder, for children, where the child is unusually likely to engage with unfamiliar adults. It requires a history of neglectful or unstable parenting that is presumed to be responsible. The behaviour cant be explained by ADHD impulsivity. Its specified as persistent if it lasts more than 12 months. Developmental age must be 9+

Pica overview

A disorder involving persistent eating of nonnutritive, nonfood substances over a period of at least 1 month. must be inappropriate to developmental level. More common in children. For adults, usually occurs alongside intellectual disability.

Rumination disorder overview

A disorder involving persistently regurgiating food (and re-chewing, re-swallowing, or spitting out) for at least 1 month. anorexia and bulimia exclude it.

avoidant/restricitve food intake disorder overview

A disorder where eating is limited by sensory discomfort, lack of interest, or fears/negative associations. Leading to nutritional defiiciency or low weight. No evidence of disturbed body-image.

anorexia nervosa overview

A disorder where energy intake is restricted and leads to significantly low weight. intense fear of becomnig fat. disturbed body-image or lack of recognition of seriousness of weight loss

anorexia nervosa subtypes

binging/purging and restricting are the two subtypes of what disorder?

bulimia nervosa overview

A disorder involving frequent binge-eating which they lack control of, and compensatory actions to prevent weight gain (vomiting, laxitives, exercise, fasting). disturbed body image. Sufferers are typically normal weight or overweight.

Main distinctions between anorexia and bulimia

Whether or not the sufferer is significantly underweight. how restrictive their typically eating is. How afraid they are of gaining weight.

Binge-eating disorder overview

A disorder involving hard-to-control binge-eating atleat once per week for 3 months. No inappropriate compensatory behaviours (e.g., purging)

Delirium Overview

A disorder that involves a reduced ability to control and sustain attention. It develops quickly (1 hour to a few days) and tends to fluctuate in severity. It must be accomanied by another cognitive deficit too (memory, language, perception, etc.), and it must be attribuatable to a medical condition or a substance. It cant occur only in a reduced arousal state (e.g., a coma)

Delirium specifiers

A disorder that specifies which medical condition or medication causes it, whether it is acute (hours-days) or persistent (weeks-months), and what level of activity accompanies it (hyperactive, hypoactive, mixed).

Major neurocognitive disorder

A disorder that involves significant cognitive decline in one or more cognitive domains, evidenced by observation (and concern) or testing. deficits must interfere with independence. Specifies etiology, presence of behavioural disturbance, and severity (level of independence)

OCD obsession definition

thoughts, images, or urges, experienced as intrusive and unwanted; which one attempts to suppress or neutralise through behaviour or mental action

OCD compulsion definition

repetitive behaviour or mental act that one feels driven to perform, with rigid rules, in response to intrusive/unwanted thought/image/urge. It must aimed at reducing anxiety or preventing a dreaded situation, and it must be either realistically unconnected to the feared outcome, or clearly excessive.
