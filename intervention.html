What is free association in psychoanalysis?A technique where clients verbalize thoughts without censorship to uncover unconscious material.
What is transference in psychoanalysis?The projection of feelings about important others onto the therapist.
What is countertransference in psychoanalysis?The therapist's emotional reaction to the client, influenced by the therapist's own history.
What does resistance refer to in psychoanalysis?Client behaviors or thoughts that hinder progress in therapy, often unconscious.
What is interpretation in psychoanalysis?The therapist explains the meaning of unconscious material to the client.

What is differentiation of self in Bowenian theory?The ability to maintain a sense of self while staying emotionally connected to family.
What does Bowenian therapy aim to change first: the individual or the family system?The family system, by addressing patterns across generations.

What is the main focus of Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT)?Building solutions based on client strengths and past successes, rather than analyzing problems.

What are exceptions in SFBT?Times when the client's problem was absent or less severe, used to identify solutions.
What is scaling in SFBT?Asking clients to rate progress or confidence on a numerical scale, typically 0–10.
